Part 2.3: Gaussian and Laplacian Stacks

For this particular task, I successfully implemented both the Gaussian Stack and the Laplacian Stack. These two techniques played a crucial role in achieving the desired results. The Gaussian Stack helped in smoothing out the image and reducing noise, while the Laplacian Stack was instrumental in splicing the band pass frequencies between the gaussian stack layers. The idea of the stacks is to use these for Laplacian stack or multiresolution blending. Combining the coarsest or lowest frequency base of the image with all the band pass layers in the laplacian stack should recover the original image theoretically, which can be seen in the second figure.
